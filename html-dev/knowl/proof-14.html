Proof

Suppose that \(e\) and \(e'\) are both identities in \(G\text{.}\) Then \(eg = ge = g\) and \(e'g = ge' = g\) for all \(g \in G\text{.}\) We need to show that \(e = e'\text{.}\) If we think of \(e\) as the identity, then \(ee' = e'\text{;}\) but if \(e'\) is the identity, then \(ee' = e\text{.}\) Combining these two equations, we have \(e = ee' = e'\text{.}\) (David Farmer helped with this proof.)
